From Coverage to Careers: An ArcGIS Storymap

West Virginia is known across the nation for its natural beauty, but the residents of the state do not recieve the first world services that the rest of the country does. While the state fails to provide adequate levels of several types of critical infrastructure, this storymap focuses on specifically broadband access in the Mountain State. As a requirement for the Geography 572 class, this site identifies patterns of economic prosperity and broadband access and persuades the reader to advocate for better funding in disadvantaged areas. You can see the full map here

Byzantine Mosaic Basemap

I love the complexity, stunning golds, and symbolic imagery of the lost Byzantine Empire. So for Lab 2 of my Geography 572 course, I wanted to honor it with a basemap design that consists of the golds, blues, and tiles that will bring the viewer back to the streets of Constantinople. You can view the full map explainer, created with CSS and HTML, here.

Lowry Hill, Minneapolis 2040 Plan Analysis.

This map was for a project in my Urban Planning 512 course. Using public data from the City of Minneapolis, my group was able to identify development trends across census tracts that were major indicators of gentrification.

Cycling Safety in California

This map was for a lab for my Geography 370 course. It utilized bike crash statistics from the State of California, normalized relative to the population of each county. The output is a map that shows the most dangerous locations to bike in the state.
